An interactive, publication-ready dashboard visualising the physicochemical and heavy-metal characterisation of surface/ground water samples collected across three study sites in the Federal Capital Territory (FCT), Abuja during the **2024 rainy season**.

## 📊 Dashboard Features

The interactive HTML dashboard (`water_quality_dashboard_2024.html`) includes:

- **KPI Summary Cards** — instant at-a-glance reading for pH, conductivity, TDS, hardness, chloride, and turbidity (Kwali site)
- **Cross-Site Physical Parameter Comparison** — grouped bar charts comparing all three sites
- **pH Distribution** — bar chart with WHO guideline range overlay
- **Conductivity vs TDS Scatter Plot** — 20 Kwali sample scatter
- **Hardness Doughnut Chart** — proportion comparison across sites
- **Multi-parameter Radar Chart** — ions (Cl⁻, NO₃⁻, NO₂⁻, salinity, turbidity) per site
- **Heavy Metals Line Chart** — Co, Fe, Pb, Cd across 20 Kwali samples
- **Major Ions Chart** — Ca, Mg, Na, K per sample
- **Statistical Summary Table** — mean values vs WHO limits with compliance status

## 📈 Key Findings

- **pH** across all three sites (6.38–6.76) falls within WHO recommended range of 6.5–8.5
- **Turbidity** is lowest at Sheda (0.81 NTU) and highest at Kwali (mean 2.71 NTU, with one outlier at 40.1 NTU)
- **TDS** is significantly elevated at Bako (mean 843 mg/L) vs Sheda (151 mg/L), though all sites remain below WHO's 1000 mg/L limit
- **Hardness** is highest at Sheda (331 mg/L), which may reflect local geology
- **Nitrite levels** at Sheda (8.92 mg/L) and Bako (10.09 mg/L) exceed the WHO guideline of 3.0 mg/L — flagged for further investigation
- **Lead (Pb)** shows notable variability across Kwali samples (range: −0.04 to 1.59 mg/L)
